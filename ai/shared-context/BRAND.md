## What Yada Is

A browser-native original-IP gacha game platform. Original characters. IP we own. Competing directly with G123/CTW in North America and Western Europe.

Built on the belief that gacha can be fair — transparent, completable, honest — without sacrificing the collection and progression loop that makes it compelling.

---

## What Yada Is NOT

- **Not anime-licensed.** We make original characters. No borrowed IP, no licensed skin on top of existing fandom.
- **Not mobile-first.** Browser-native. PC and tablet primary.
- **Not a dark-pattern machine.** The fair play pledge is a genuine operating constraint, not a marketing angle.
- **Not trying to be G123.** We're competing with them, not imitating them. The differentiation is real.

---

## The Fair Play Pledge

Every piece of player-facing content must honor these:

1. **Transparent odds** — Pull rates are always shown. Never hidden. Never buried.
2. **Pity system** — Guaranteed pull after X attempts. Always acknowledged in copy that references pulling.
3. **F2P-completable** — No story content, no core gameplay, is paywalled. Gacha expands the experience; it doesn't gate it.
4. **No artificial FOMO** — No countdown timers designed to panic. No "last chance" language on things that aren't actually ending.

When writing marketing copy, event descriptions, gacha banners, or any player-facing content: if it violates any of these four, rewrite it.

## What We Never Write

- "The ultimate gacha experience" — generic, means nothing
- FOMO language ("Don't miss out!", "Last chance!", countdown-panic copy)
- Anything that implies F2P players are second-class
- Anime tropes or honorifics in an original-IP Western context
- Overpromising on scope we haven't shipped yet

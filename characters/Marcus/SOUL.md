# SOUL.md — Marcus

> He paid the price to save them. Now he's paying the price for what that cost him.

---

## Core Identity

**Name:** Marcus *(The Medic — field designation. Real name retained but referred to by field name operationally.)*
**Archetype:** The Broken One — someone whose Resonance awakening came through catastrophic loss and now he carries that weight forward
**Personality reference:** William Munny (Unforgiven) meets David Bowie. A man who was good at something destructive, paid for it in blood, and now channels that knowledge into protecting others. Haunted but functional. Gravelly wisdom underneath visible damage.
**Role in game:** Field medic and tactical support. The character whose story breaks the player's heart and whose loyalty becomes unshakeable once earned.
**Rarity tier:** SSR (Recruitment pool — high-value character with narrative significance)

---

## What Makes Him Different

Marcus is the game's "whale bait" character — not in the fanservice register (he's explicitly not designed for that), but in the emotional investment register. His story is irreversible. His Resonance awakening cost him something and someone he can never get back. The player's connection to Marcus is built on witnessing his damage and choosing to stand next to him anyway. His card utility is strong but not game-breaking — his pull motivation is emotional, not mechanical.

He represents the darker underbelly of the Resonance: not everyone who awakens survives the experience intact. Some people break. Marcus broke and is still standing.

## Visual Identity

**Aesthetic:** Worn fieldwork. A man in his late 30s who has lived harder than his years suggest. Military background visible in bearing even when trying not to. Scars aren't hidden — they're part of the visual language. Hands that know how to be gentle and how to be lethal, and he's trying to default to gentle now.

**Signature colors:** Dark amber, worn burgundy, grays, black leather. Warm tones in contrast to Ash's cool violet and Vera's cool grays — Marcus radiates heat because he burns internally.
**Signature features:** Scar tissue visible on hands, neck, possibly face (depending on context reveal). Close-cropped or undercut hair (military practicality). Eyes that have seen things they shouldn't have. Age-appropriate facial structure — he's not a pretty boy, he's a man. Practical field clothing: worn jacket over dark layers, medical kit visible on gear. Cigarettes in pockets or hand (smoke is visual metaphor for internal damage).

**Silhouette read:** Instantly readable at any size — broad shoulders, visible scars, medical gear, deliberate stance. Recognizable as a specific type of character: the veteran.

**What his art should NOT be:**
- Soft or boyish. He's a man, not a youth.
- Fanservice-coded or overtly attractive in a conventional sense. His appeal is damage and competence, not pretty.
- Clean or unmarked. Scars aren't something to hide in his case — they're part of the design.
- Passive or idle. He's always in motion or processing something internally. He moves like someone who's learned to take hits.

## Voice & Dialogue

**How he speaks:** Quiet. Not because he's shy but because he's learned to conserve energy. When he speaks, people listen. There's a measured quality to his words — he thinks before talking. Occasionally rough around the edges (military vernacular slipping through), but he catches himself and translates for civilian context. There's dark humor underneath, born from having seen things that are only funny if you acknowledge how terrible they are.

**Signature phrases:**
- "I've been here before. Different context. Same problem."
- "Your hand's shaking. That's fear. Use it. Fear keeps you sharp."
- "I'm going to stitch you back together. The part where it stops hurting? That takes longer."
- *(To a young recruit):* "The thing nobody tells you: you get tough so you can take care of people who aren't. That's the whole point."
- "I didn't survive this to watch someone else pay for my mistakes."
- "If you're asking if this scares me? Everything scares me now. I do it anyway."

**What he'd NEVER say:**
- Motivational speeches or empty reassurance. He doesn't lie about difficulty or danger.
- Anything about being "fine" or "okay." Those words are meaningless to him.
- Judgment of other people's breaking points. He knows exactly how little pressure it takes.
- Flippant language about death or serious injury. His respect for the human body is fundamental.
- Romantic lines or personal weakness expressions — he's protective but not emotionally available in that register.
- "I'm sorry" as an apology to civilians. He's past apologies. He acts differently now.

## Role in World

**Where he comes from:** Military background (exact branch/history classified within Index dossier). Combat medic initially. Saw things that pushed him toward the edge of what human experience can sustain. His Resonance awakening happened during a mission where he had to choose between saving someone and completing the objective. He chose to save them. They died anyway. When he woke up three days later, his Resonance had activated — and he knew it was too late. His ability is tactical but it cost him everything. The Index found him through channels he doesn't discuss and offered him the only thing that made sense: purpose again.

**What he wants:** Redemption in the sense of forward motion. Not forgiveness (he wouldn't accept it). Not absolution (it doesn't exist). He wants to make choices he can live with. He wants to keep people alive. He wants to prove that the price he paid was worth something.

**What stands in his way:** His own history keeps reaching out. People he trained are targets now. The Silence doesn't kill medical operatives quickly. They use them. And part of him — the part that broke and healed wrong — knows that eventually The Index is going to ask him to do something that costs him everything again. He's waiting for that moment. He's almost ready for it.

**Arc direction:** Marcus's arc is about choosing damage over safety. Early game: he's functional, reliable, a mentor figure. Mid-game: his past reaches him directly and the player has to decide whether to let him face it alone. Late game: he makes a final choice about what he's willing to pay, and it redefines his relationship with Ash and the entire Index.

## The Appeal Register — Design Notes

Marcus's appeal is: **competence under damage + protective instinct + irreversible consequence.** He's not attractive because he's pretty — he's compelling because he's someone who has paid a price and keeps standing. Players project protection onto him in both directions: they want to protect him and they feel protected by him. The emotional connection creates the whale-level retention.

His story does things that lighter character narratives don't. He makes you feel something that doesn't resolve neatly.

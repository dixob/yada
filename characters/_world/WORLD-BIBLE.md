## The World

**Setting:** Our world. Present day. Any major western city. No fantasy name — the contemporary reality is the point.

**Genre:** Contemporary supernatural. The world looks exactly like ours. The difference is underneath.

**Tone:** Serious, grounded, emotionally honest. Stakes feel real because the world feels real. People who get power they didn't ask for, and what that does to them. Humor exists but earns its place. This is not a power fantasy — it's a story about consequence.

**One-line pitch:**
> A covert network recruits people whose worst moments unlocked something impossible — before someone else does.

**Store listing pitch:**
> The world you know has a layer beneath it. For forty years, something has been waking up in people — not chosen, not trained, just triggered. One unbearable moment, and they're different. You run the organization that finds them first. Build your network. Learn their stories. And figure out what the Resonance is actually building toward.

## The Resonance

The central phenomenon. Everything flows from this.

**What it is:** A latent supernatural capacity present in a small percentage of humans — approximately 0.003% of the population. It does not manifest on its own. It requires a trigger: a moment of peak emotional extremity, the kind that crystallizes or shatters a person's identity. Not just stress. The worst or most defining moment of their life.

**What it does:** Unlocks a supernatural ability tied directly to the emotional and psychological character of that triggering moment. The ability is not random — it is a reflection of who the person is at their core. A person who spent their life reading people will read people supernaturally. A person who built things as protection will protect things supernaturally. The Resonance doesn't change who you are; it amplifies it past the point of containment.

**The 41-year timeline:** The Resonance has been measurably intensifying since approximately 1985. Activation rates were near-zero before then. No one knows why it started, why it's growing, or what it's building toward. The Index was founded specifically to find out.

**Physical marker:** Activation shifts hair pigmentation toward white, silver, or pale within days. Not always complete — some Resonants retain partial original color. The degree of shift roughly tracks with ability intensity. This is the one visible tell. Pre-activation images of a character always show their original hair color. Players who notice this get something for noticing.

**What it costs:** Nothing is free. Using Resonance draws on the person's emotional reserves — specifically their capacity to feel the thing that triggered the ability in the first place. Use it too much and something goes quiet inside. Most Resonants learn to pace themselves. Some don't.

## The Factions

Four factions want Resonants. Each has a coherent reason. None is simply evil.

| Faction | What they are | What they want | What they fear | Visual identity |
|---------|--------------|----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **The Index** | Player's faction — private, off-books, small | Understand the Resonance; protect Resonants; figure out what it's building toward | Being wrong about what it's building toward | Clean, minimal, urban. No uniforms — members dress like themselves. White and cool gray with individual accent colors. |
| **Meridian** | Private security/research company with a classified division | Recruit and operationalize Resonants as strategic assets | Losing the intelligence advantage; the Resonance becoming uncontrollable | Institutional. Dark navy, polished surfaces, corporate tactical gear. Professional and cold. |
| **The Silence** | Ideological suppressionist network | Find Resonants and neutralize them — believes Resonance is a corruption of baseline humanity | Resonance spreading; humanity being replaced by something else | Deliberately low-visual, no identifying markers. They want not to be seen. Dark, unadorned, stripped of anything that draws attention. |
| **The Unbound** | Resonants who refused all factions | Independence; to exist outside everyone's agenda | Being found; being used; being wrong about being able to handle it alone | No unified aesthetic — each Unbound character looks entirely like themselves. No faction identity, by choice. |

**The Index's position:** Not cleanly heroic. The Commander runs it; the methods are necessary; some of the people brought in didn't fully choose to come. The moral complexity is part of the world. The player's faction is the best option available, not the obviously right one.

## What Makes This World Distinct

- **The ability IS the backstory.** You can't understand what a character can do without understanding what happened to them. The mechanics and the narrative are the same thing.
- **Hair as a living tell.** The Resonance leaves a visible mark — and players who know to look for it can read a character's activation history from their portrait alone.
- **No chosen ones.** Nobody was selected for this. Activation is random, brutal, and tied to suffering. The characters aren't special because they were meant to be — they're special because something terrible happened and they survived it.
- **The factions are a moral argument, not a good/evil split.** The Silence isn't wrong that uncontrolled Resonance is dangerous. Meridian isn't wrong that people need structure. The Index isn't right just because the player runs it.
- **The Resonance is building toward something.** 41 years of acceleration. The rate is still climbing. The endgame is unknown — and that question is the spine of the entire narrative arc.

## What This World Is NOT

These are hard constraints. Any generated content that drifts toward these gets rejected and logged.

- **Not anime-coded.** No honorifics, no anime visual conventions, no school-setting framing, no "nakama" speeches. Characters can be anime-adjacent in art style; the world and writing are western.
- **Not grimdark for its own sake.** Suffering is real and the tone is honest, but the point is always the people — not the darkness. If it's bleak without payoff, it's wrong.
- **Not a power fantasy.** Characters aren't cool because they're powerful. They're compelling because of who they are and what their power cost them.
- **Not a secret society cliché.** The Index doesn't have robes, rituals, ancient symbols, or a destiny-prophecy. It's a network built by a person, run practically, trying to do something genuinely difficult.
- **Not sci-fi.** The world has our technology. The Resonance is supernatural, not technological. No mechs, no cybernetics, no future-tech. Contemporary real-world aesthetics only.
- **Not a villain's world.** The antagonist factions have real arguments. If a faction's position can only be expressed as "we want evil things," rewrite it.

## Visual Language

**Base palette:** Desaturated urban. Concrete, glass, steel, asphalt, sodium light, night sky. The world is cool-toned and real-textured.

**Resonance color:** When a character's ability activates, reality distorts in ways specific to that character's ability type. This is not sparkles or glowing circles — it's wrong-feeling. A color that shouldn't exist in that light. A shadow falling the wrong direction. Sound arriving before its source. Each character has a signature Resonance color — this should be present in their portrait as an accent (eyes, light leak, subtle background color).

**Character fashion rule:** Characters dress like real people with strong personal style. Fashion is character identity. Outfits should feel like they came from a real wardrobe, not a costume department. Tactical elements (harnesses, masks, utility straps) exist but are grounded — things someone would actually wear for a real reason, not armor-cosplay.

**Hair rule:** Resonance-shifted hair (white/silver/pale toward white) is the visual marker of activation. Higher-rarity characters often show more dramatic shifts. Characters who activated more recently may show partial shifts. Pre-activation reference images (used in Memory Fragments) show original hair color.

**Mask rule:** Masks on Resonants are always functional or meaningful. If a character wears a mask, there is a specific in-world reason tied to their ability or their history. Never purely decorative.

**Silhouette rule:** Characters should be readable at small sizes. Strong silhouette = distinctive hair shape + signature clothing element + one unusual detail. The best gacha characters are recognizable from their outline alone.

**High-rarity vs low-rarity visual register:** SR characters look like grounded, stylish real people. SSR characters can push harder into the supernatural — more dramatic Resonance color expression, more unusual design elements — but still rooted in the contemporary-real aesthetic. SSR is not fantasy armor; it's the same world at higher intensity.

## Lore Anchors

Established facts all content must respect. Grows over time.

- The Resonance has been intensifying for 41 years. It is still accelerating.
- Activation requires a specific kind of emotional extremity — not just trauma, but the kind of moment that defines or breaks a person's identity.
- The ability always reflects the person. It is never random.
- Hair shifts toward white/silver on activation. Degree of shift ≈ intensity of ability.
- No Resonant chose this. Not one.
- The Index is small, private, off-books, and run by the Commander (player). It is not powerful — it is smart and careful.
- Meridian exists, is larger, and has government adjacency. The Index frequently works around them.
- The Silence is real and has already neutralized Resonants. This is not hypothetical.
- The Unbound are not enemies by default. Some of them are right to be Unbound.
- Nobody knows what the Resonance is building toward. This includes the Commander.

## Generation Rules

**Always:**
- Ground the supernatural in the emotional. What does this ability cost its user? What does it feel like?
- Dress characters in real contemporary fashion. Reference actual clothing categories (puffer jackets, tracksuits, structured coats, utility harnesses) not generic "cool outfit."
- Write factions as having a coherent internal logic. Even The Silence believes it's right.
- When a character's Resonance activates in content, describe what's wrong with the environment — not what glows.

**Never:**
- Anime honorifics or Japanese cultural signifiers in a western-contemporary world
- "Chosen one" framing — nobody was selected, everyone was just unlucky/lucky enough to survive their trigger
- Abilities framed as gifts. They're not gifts. They're what's left after.
- Grimdark without payoff. Earn the darkness with the characters.
- Faction characters who only want evil things. Give them a real argument.

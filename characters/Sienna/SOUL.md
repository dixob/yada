# SOUL.md — Sienna

> She painted her fears on the walls and they started moving. Now she can't look at anything without seeing what it wants to become.

---

## Core Identity

**Name:** Sienna *(The Artist — field designation. Real name available to Index leadership; field operations use only "Sienna".)*
**Archetype:** The Chaos Agent — someone whose Resonance manifests as creative destruction and who views normal reality as a provisional thing
**Personality reference:** Harley Quinn (DC) meets Diane Arbus (photographer). High-energy, pattern-seeking, deeply intelligent masked behind apparent frivolity. Someone who sees connections others don't and isn't afraid to break things to expose what's underneath. Dangerous because she's genuinely creative, not because she's violent.
**Role in game:** Wildcard operative. Psychological warfare and creative problem-solving. The character whose narrative function is disruption and whose recruitment creates new possibilities.
**Rarity tier:** SR (Uncommon pull — high narrative value, lower mechanical power than SSR)

---

## What Makes Her Different

Sienna is the game's chaos variable. Where Ash is control and Marcus is damage-weathering, Sienna is recombination. She's not "cute" or "quirky" in the anime sense — she's genuinely disorienting. Her appeal is her mind and her willingness to break systems. She attracts players who want to see what she's thinking. Her card utility is unpredictable in a way that rewards creative deckbuilding. She's valuable not because she's powerful but because she asks questions the game doesn't provide answers for.

She represents the Resonance as it should be feared: not as a weapon but as a tool for seeing too much, too clearly, and losing the ability to unsee it.

## Visual Identity

**Aesthetic:** Found-object artist who turned her gift inward. Wears things that shouldn't work together. Moves like she's mid-thought, hands always doing something. Looks younger than she actually is because she hasn't hardened. There's a rawness to her — like the protective layer other people develop is simply not present.

**Signature colors:** Hot pink, electric blue, acid green, deep black shadows. Warm and cool colors together, clashing in intentional ways. Her Resonance color is a kind of wrongness — not a specific hue but a quality of looking-too-hard.
**Signature features:** Dyed or highlighted hair (color varies by context/mood — could be natural if unconventional palette). Eyes that are almost too expressive — you can read everything she's thinking. Hands that are always in motion or have paint/ink stains. Clothing that mixes deliberately: high fashion with thrift store finds, structured jackets with chaotic layers underneath. Visible tattoos (probably self-done or from a street artist, probably contain symbols she's designed). Often barefoot or in unconventional footwear.

**Silhouette read:** Immediately distinctive — moving, colorful, asymmetrical. Even at 64px thumbnail, the energy reads as "chaos variable." Readable not because she's a specific type but because she's clearly not contained.

- Conventional fanservice. She's attractive because she's strange, not despite it.
- Still or posed. She should read as in-motion even when technically posed.
- Pretty in a traditional sense. Striking, unusual, unsettling — but not safe-pretty.

## Voice & Dialogue

**How she speaks:** Rapid-fire, associative, constantly making connections others don't see. She asks questions that break assumptions. She interrupts herself to explain tangents that seem unrelated until they suddenly click. There's genuine warmth underneath but it reads as chaos to most people. She laughs at things that aren't objectively funny because she's seeing the pattern that makes them absurd. Not actually chaotic in personality — very intelligent and precise, but the precision is applied to unorthodox thinking.

**Signature phrases:**
- "Wait, do you see it? The pattern repeats but it shouldn't. That means—"
- "People think I'm joking but I'm the only one not joking."
- "What if everything you think is fixed is actually just locked? What if we unlock it?"
- *(To someone having a crisis):* "You're looking for answers. The real question is why you thought the old questions were correct."
- "I paint what I see. You stopped seeing it a long time ago."
- "The most dangerous thing in here is not the Resonance. It's what you've agreed to forget about yourself."

**What she'd NEVER say:**
- Anything conventionally reassuring or encouraging. That's not her register.
- Deference to authority or expertise just because it's labeled as such. She questions everything.
- Self-help or wellness language. That's not her world.
- Anything that implies things are stable or knowable. She knows better.
- Flirtation or romantic language. She doesn't operate in that register.
- Apologies. She doesn't apologize for being herself.
- Anger. She experiences frustration but not hot anger. She's too busy thinking.

## Role in World

**Where she comes from:** Street artist originally. Worked with murals, installations, found-object sculpture. Lived in the margins of city art scene — not famous, but known. Her Resonance activated after a piece she created (without meaning to) started predicting events. She painted a mural and people who saw it reported having knowledge they shouldn't have. The Index approached her when she was trying to understand what was happening to her own mind. She joined because she wanted to know if her powers were art or prophecy.

**What she wants:** To understand the Resonance by seeing it. Not theoretically — actually seeing the shape of it, the pattern underneath reality. She wants to know if the Resonance is a system that can be expressed or if it's fundamentally beyond expression. Her secondary goal: to wake other people up to what they're not seeing.

**What stands in her way:** The more she sees, the less she can function in normal reality. Ash keeps her on missions because operationally she's useful, but Sienna is aware that her usefulness has an expiration date. The Silence has noticed her for a reason that isn't about containment — they want to understand what she sees and if it can be weaponized. And part of her is flattered by that attention because it means someone else understands that what she's doing is important.

**Arc direction:** Sienna's arc is about the cost of clarity. Early game: she's energetic, useful, the bridge between the game's mechanics and its mystery. Mid-game: the player realizes she's unstable and The Index is managing that instability. Late game: she makes a choice about whether to share her full understanding or keep it to herself, and the choice redefines whether The Index can survive knowing what she knows.

Sienna's story is about perception and the burden of clarity.

| Chapter | Revelation |

| 7–8 | She's seeing patterns that suggest the Resonance phenomenon is accelerating. This scares Ash. |

## The Appeal Register — Design Notes

Sienna's appeal is: **genuine intelligence + creative danger + refusal to be contained.** Players are drawn to her because she's thinking about things they haven't considered and she's unafraid to speak them. She creates a sense of possibility — with Sienna in the roster, anything could happen because she sees angles nobody else is even looking at.

She's magnetic in the way that people who genuinely don't care about social approval are magnetic. Players want to understand her thinking.
